About Me

My name is Harold Doran and I have been working in measurement for 20 years and prior to that worked in K-12 as a teacher and a school principal for 8 years. I have always had a passion for developing tools that statisticians, psychometricians, and data scientists can use to make their work more efficient. It's driven mainly by my own curiosity to explore psychometric and statistical techniques at their core to make sure that I understand them better myself. I often find that the detailed process of writing code reveals much about the methods we routinely use.

I think of software development as part of my artistic expression and use it as my palette to make ideas open and accessible. I think a lot about building scalable computational infrastructure in two ways. First, the algorithms at their core must be efficient and scalable, a topic I have written about here. Second, we can then scale up our systems using modern computing infrastructures like cloud-based computing and taking advantage of distrbuted computing concepts. My personal opinion is we often focus on the latter and ignore the former. Maybe my biggest passion is music and playing on stage.
